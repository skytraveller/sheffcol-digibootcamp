4.2
Describe the tests used for functional and non-functional requirements.






Functional requirements describe ways a product must behave
Functional requirements: calculations, technical details, data manipulation, data processing.
Functional testing is based on the business requirement.

Nonfunctional requirements, also known as quality attributes, describe the general software characteristics
Non-functional requirements: accessibility, efficiency, reliability, scalability, maintainability, security, robustness.
Non-functional testing is based on the performance requirement.
